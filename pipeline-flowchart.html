How they aggregate

All three sources are capture adapters over one shared core. Only capture & engage are platform-specific; everything below is shared:

  • One judge — the same significance rubric (judge_prompt.md) decides SIG/INSIG/SKIP (and "valuable" for video content) on every source.
  • Two registries — people-db is the human identity layer and the X/LinkedIn watchlist; channels.json is the YouTube watchlist. Discovery grows them, and they feed the next run's scans (the == scanned next run ==> loop).
  • One dedup store — shared seen-ids mean a post/video caught by one path is never re-reported by another.
  • One digest — significant items from all three converge into the dated report (per-person for X/LinkedIn, per-video for YouTube).
  • Bounding — every "new?" 분기 can answer no → empty run; all caps are ceilings, never targets.

Status: X & LinkedIn (watchlist + home) and YouTube (channel-scan + discovery) are built; engagement is dry-run-verified (not flipped live) on X/LinkedIn and deferred on YouTube; the video-"valuable" criterion is still a deferred stand-in.
